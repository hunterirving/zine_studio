In 1040 AD, Chinese inventor Bi Sheng carved individual characters into clay pieces, creating the world's first movable type printing system. Four centuries later, Johannes Gutenberg developed his own movable type system using metal letters and a mechanical press, revolutionizing European society and spreading literacy across the Western world. Though inkjet and laser printers now sit in homes around the world, few use them to become publishers themselves.
